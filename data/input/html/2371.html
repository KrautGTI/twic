Fame is a fickle food
The Poems of Emily Dickinson, Variorum Edition, Franklin, 1998
Fame is a fickle food
Upon a shifting plate
Whose table once a
Guest but not
The second time is set
Whose crumbs the crows inspect
And with ironic caw
Flap past it to the
Farmer's corn
Men eat of it and die
