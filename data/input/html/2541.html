Whose cheek is this?
The Poems of Emily Dickinson, Johnson, 1955
Whose cheek is this?
What rosy face
Has lost a blush today?
I found her -- `pleiad' -- in the woods
And bore her safe away.
Robins, in the tradition
Did cover such with leaves,
But which the cheek --
And which the pall
My scrutiny deceives.
