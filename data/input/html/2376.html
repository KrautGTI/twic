Judgment is justest
The Poems of Emily Dickinson, Variorum Edition, Franklin, 1998
Judgment is justest
When the Judged
His action laid away
Divested is of every Disk
But his sincerity
Honor is then the safest hue
In a posthumous Sun
Not any color will endure
That scrutiny can burn.
