The road to Paradise is plain
The Poems of Emily Dickinson, Variorum Edition, Franklin, 1998
The road to Paradise is plain
And holds scarce one
Not that it is not firm
But we presume
A dappled road
Is more preferred.
The throngs of Paradise are few.
Not me - nor you -
But unsuspected things
Mines have no wings.
